01

Four-Bar Linkage — Container Challenge

Mechanism Synthesis Kinematics Linkage Design
+

Overview

Designed a four-bar linkage to automate a trash container tray. The tray starts flat on top (position A), moves out for user deposit, then rotates to dump contents into a rear opening (position B) — without the linkage crossing the container interior. Pivots constrained to container side walls only.

Requirements

  • Tray moves from top (A) to side dump position (B)
  • Pivot points on container sides only, no extensions
  • Linkage must not traverse the container interior
  • Positions A and B are the extremes of motion

Key Dimensions

Top opening35 cm (15 + 5 + 15)
Vertical drop A → B15 cm
Side opening height5 cm from base
Pivot constraintSide walls only

Deliverables

  • Graphical two-position synthesis
  • Ground pivot locations and all link lengths
  • Verification of motion extremes at A and B
  • Coupler path trace through full range of motion

02

Motor Speed Control — Unstable Load Transport

Motor Control Arduino Dynamic Simulation Prototype
+

Overview

Designed and built a motor-driven cart that transports a 1-foot vertical aluminum bar (8020 extrusion) across a variable distance (5–10 ft) and back, stop to stop, without toppling. The bar rests on the platform base only — no fasteners or side supports. Objective was minimum transport time.

Requirements

  • Transport 1 ft bar stop-to-stop, distance randomized at competition
  • Bar must stand at rest at end of trial
  • Arduino control sketch uploaded day-of
  • Wheel diameter optimized from simulation

Hardware

Actuator12V DC gear motor
ControllerArduino UNO + power amp
Load8020 1010 extrusion, 1 ft
Structure3D print + wood transmission

Approach

  • SolidWorks multibody sim to find minimum time
  • Analytical stability condition as baseline check
  • Trapezoidal velocity profile on Arduino
  • Wheel diameter balancing torque and speed

Reflection
